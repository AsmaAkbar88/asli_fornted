# Glossary of Terms

This glossary provides definitions for key terms used throughout the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics book.

## A

**Actuator**: A mechanical component that moves or controls a mechanism or system by converting various forms of energy into motion.

**AI (Artificial Intelligence)**: The simulation of human intelligence processes by machines, especially computer systems.

**Autonomous System**: A system that operates independently without human intervention.

## B

**Behavior Tree**: A mathematical model of plan execution used in robotics and artificial intelligence to control the behavior of autonomous agents.

**Bayesian Network**: A probabilistic graphical model that represents a set of variables and their conditional dependencies via a directed acyclic graph.

## C

**Computer Vision**: A field of artificial intelligence that trains computers to interpret and understand the visual world.

**Convolutional Neural Network (CNN)**: A class of deep neural networks, most commonly applied to analyzing visual imagery.

**Cloud Robotics**: A field of robotics that attempts to invoke cloud technologies for robotics.

## D

**Deep Learning**: A subset of machine learning based on artificial neural networks with representation learning.

**Distributed System**: A system whose components are located on different networked computers, which communicate and coordinate their actions by passing messages to one another.

**Dynamics**: The study of forces and torques and their effect on motion.

## E

**Embodied AI**: Artificial intelligence that involves a physical body interacting with the real world.

**Encoder**: A device that measures the motion of a shaft or axle, to determine position, angle, or velocity.

**End Effector**: The device at the end of a robotic arm, designed to interact with the environment.

## F

**Forward Kinematics**: The use of kinematic equations to compute the position of the end-effector from specified values of joint parameters.

**Force Control**: A control strategy that regulates the forces exerted by a robot's end effector on the environment.

**Field of View (FOV)**: The extent of the observable world that is seen at any given moment.

## G

**Gazebo**: An open-source 3D robotics simulator that enables accurate and efficient testing of robots.

**Gaussian Process**: A stochastic process, a collection of random variables, any finite number of which have a joint Gaussian distribution.

**GPU (Graphics Processing Unit)**: A specialized electronic circuit designed to rapidly manipulate and alter memory to accelerate the creation of images in a frame buffer intended for output to a display device.

## H

**Hardware-in-the-Loop (HIL)**: A simulation technique that connects real hardware to a simulation environment.

**Heuristic**: Any approach to problem solving that employs a practical method that is not guaranteed to be optimal, perfect, or rational, but is nevertheless sufficient for reaching an immediate goal.

**Human-Robot Interaction (HRI)**: A field of study dedicated to understanding, designing, and evaluating robotic systems for human interaction.

## I

**Inverse Kinematics**: The process of determining the joint parameters that provide a desired position of the end-effector.

**IoT (Internet of Things)**: A system of interrelated computing devices, mechanical and digital machines provided with unique identifiers and the ability to transfer data over a network without requiring human-to-human or human-to-computer interaction.

**IMU (Inertial Measurement Unit)**: An electronic device that measures and reports a body's specific force, angular rate, and sometimes the magnetic field surrounding the body.

## J

**Joint Space**: The space defined by the joint angles of a robotic manipulator.

**Jacobian Matrix**: A matrix of all first-order partial derivatives of a vector-valued function.

## K

**Kinematics**: The study of motion without considering the forces that cause it.

**Kalman Filter**: An algorithm that uses a series of measurements observed over time, containing statistical noise and other inaccuracies, and produces estimates of unknown variables that tend to be more accurate than those based on a single measurement alone.

## L

**LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging)**: A remote sensing method that uses light in the form of a pulsed laser to measure distances.

**Localization**: The process of determining the position and orientation of a robot in a known or unknown environment.

**Logistic Regression**: A statistical model that in its basic form uses a logistic function to model a binary dependent variable.

## M

**Machine Learning**: The study of computer algorithms that can improve automatically through experience and by the use of data.

**Manipulation**: The capability of a robot to physically interact with objects in its environment.

**Monte Carlo Method**: A broad class of computational algorithms that rely on repeated random sampling to obtain numerical results.

## N

**Neural Network**: A series of algorithms that endeavors to recognize underlying relationships in a set of data through a process that mimics the way the human brain operates.

**Natural Language Processing (NLP)**: A subfield of linguistics, computer science, and artificial intelligence concerned with the interactions between computers and human language.

**Navigation**: The process of planning, recording, and controlling the movement of a robot from one location to another.

## O

**Occupancy Grid**: A probabilistic 2D or 3D representation of space that divides the world into discrete cells and assigns each cell a probability of being occupied.

**OpenCV**: An open-source computer vision and machine learning software library.

**Odometry**: The use of data from motion sensors to estimate change in position over time.

## P

**Path Planning**: The computational process of finding a valid sequence of configurations to move an object from a source to a destination.

**PID Controller**: A control loop mechanism employing feedback that is widely used in industrial control systems.

**Perception**: The ability of a robot to sense and interpret its environment.

## Q

**Quaternion**: A mathematical concept used to represent rotations and orientations in 3D space.

**Q-Learning**: A model-free reinforcement learning algorithm to learn quality of actions telling an agent what action to take under what circumstances.

## R

**ROS (Robot Operating System)**: Flexible framework for writing robot software that provides services designed for a heterogeneous computer cluster.

**Reinforcement Learning**: An area of machine learning concerned with how intelligent agents ought to take actions in an environment in order to maximize the notion of cumulative reward.

**Robotics Middleware**: Software that provides programmatic interfaces for developing and connecting robotics applications.

## S

**SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping)**: The computational problem of constructing or updating a map of an unknown environment while simultaneously keeping track of an agent's location within it.

**Sensor Fusion**: The combining of sensory data or data derived from disparate sources such that the resulting information has less uncertainty than would be possible when these sources were used individually.

**State Space**: The set of all possible configurations of a system.

## T

**TensorFlow**: An end-to-end open source platform for machine learning.

**Teaching Pendant**: A handheld device used to teach or program a robot.

**Trajectory**: A path that a moving object follows through space as a function of time.

## V

**VLA (Vision-Language-Action)**: A unified model that combines visual perception, natural language understanding, and motor control.

**Vision System**: A system that uses cameras and image processing to provide visual feedback to a robot.

**Virtual Reality (VR)**: A simulated experience that can be similar to or completely different from the real world.

## W

**Waypoint**: A reference or marker for a location used for navigation.

**Workspace**: The space within which a robot manipulator can operate.

## Y

**Yaw**: Rotation around the vertical axis of a vehicle or robot.

## Z

**Zero Moment Point (ZMP)**: A concept used in robotics and biomechanics to aid in the analysis and control of legged locomotion.
